Olga & Bruce McKenzie

Fly to the Limit is owned and operated by Olga and Bruce McKenzie. Both born in Scotland, travelled extensively, both have commercial pilors licenses with 19 and 9 years respectively flying for middle eastern airlines.

They own and operate a Cessna Caravan Amphibian which can take up to 7 people to any remote lake in the area. They also own a DG Flugzeugbau DG-1001S with a Cessna Turbo Stationair to tow it. The Cessna is also available for custom scenic tours.There are currently 4 fixed-wing aircraft, 1 glider (soarer), and 1 helicopter.

Every plane is owner operated, with safety and maintenance the highest priority. All of their staff are trained in sustainable tourism and share a love of the mountains and our peaceful, natural scenery.

Finn McCool

Stunt pilot with the Red Arrows (UK fairforce stunt team), has served in combat chopper in 3 recent wars, and fears nothing except small dogs and single women. Owns an Extra EA-200 for the ultimate full stunt flight experience, and flies all our other fixed wing craft much more sedately when required.

Wendy "Dropper" Gilhallen

13 years commercial pilot in Africa, Russia and South America, during which she survived 3 crashes (none her own fault, she maintain). Owns a Cessna-172Skyhawk P that is ideal for low level sight seeing, rides a Harley and is a ski instructor during the season.

John "Doc" Holliday

With 10,000+ hours piloting helicopters in the bush & mountains of the Southern Alps for deer recovery and mountain rescue operations, Doc is well qualified to land you and your friends in remote parts of the country that only he knows about. He’ll help you plot your route, drop extra provisions where you want them, and pick you up when you’re done.

Hans Gerhansen

Hans has flown almost everything there is to fly. He spent his younger years being a test pilot but left that because he prefers company while in the air. Hans owns a microlight and can be seen behind the pilot seat of any of our aircraft when he’s not skiing, tramping, climbing, hang-gliding, or performing with his band at a local hotel.
